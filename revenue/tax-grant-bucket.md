## Tax Refund Opportunities

| Item | Est. Value | Action Required | Notes |
|------|-----------|----------------|-------|
| **Home Office Deduction (Simplified Method)** | $300–$1,500/yr | File Schedule C + Form 8829 or use simplified ($5/sqft × dedicated space, max 300sqft = $1,500) | Requires exclusive-use dedicated space; available now for 2025 return |
| **Home Office Deduction (Regular Method)** | $800–$3,000/yr | Calculate % of home used for business × actual expenses (rent, utilities, internet) | Higher value for NYC renters paying $2K+/mo rent |
| **Self-Employment Business Expenses** | $500–$720/yr | Deduct on Schedule C | API costs ($39–60/mo), hosting/Ampere.sh, domain registration — all deductible. ~$600/yr at current burn |
| **Self-Employment Tax Deduction (SE tax ½)** | ~50% of SE tax paid | Automatic on Schedule 1 | Deduct half of SE tax from gross income — immediate, no filing complexity |
| **Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction — Sec. 199A** | Up to 20% of net business income | File Form 8995 with Schedule C | Applies to sole proprietors; worth $0 if no net income yet BUT stacks when revenue starts. Expires Dec 31, 2025 under TCJA (potential extension watch) |
| **R&D Tax Credit — Section 41 (Form 6765)** | Up to 20% of qualified R&D spend | File Form 6765 | AI development qualifies as QRE (qualified research expenditures). Startup offset: can apply credit against PAYROLL TAX (up to $500K/yr) — key for pre-profit. Requires records of time/expenses |
| **R&D Payroll Tax Offset (Startup provision)** | Up to $500K/yr against payroll tax | Form 6765 + Form 941 | New startup provision: R&D credit can offset employer payroll tax even with $0 income tax. Activated once EIN + payroll exist |
| **Self-Employed Health Insurance Deduction** | 100% of premiums paid | Schedule 1, Line 17 | Deduct 100% of health insurance premiums paid if not covered by employer plan. Check if applicable |
| **SEP-IRA Contribution (Retirement deduction)** | Up to 25% of net self-employment income | Open SEP-IRA account, contribute before tax deadline | Reduces taxable income dollar-for-dollar; invest in retirement while reducing taxes |
| **NYC Unincorporated Business Tax (UBT) — Exemption** | Avoid UBT entirely | Ensure net income stays under $95K threshold | NYC UBT exemption: first $95K net income exempt. Pre-revenue = $0 UBT owed |
| **NY State Pass-Through Entity Tax (PTET) Credit** | Variable | Elect PTET on NY return | NY State PTET can give federal deduction on state taxes paid — useful once revenue flows |
| **Amended Returns (1040-X) — Missed deductions** | $200–$2,000+ potential | File 1040-X for 2022, 2023, 2024 | If prior years had home office or SE expenses not claimed — 3-year lookback window open through 2025/2026/2027 |
| **Business Startup Costs Deduction (Sec. 195)** | Up to $5,000 in Year 1 | Include on Schedule C | First $5,000 of startup costs deductible in year business launches; remainder amortized over 15 years |
