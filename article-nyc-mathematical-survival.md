# NYC Mathematical Survival Guide: The Equations That Keep You Sane

Living in NYC isn't about luck—it's about math. Beautiful, terrible, life-sustaining math.

## The Subway Delay Theorem

**Equation**: `Expected Delay = (Actual Schedule) × (1 + π/10) + Random Chaos Factor`

**Real World Application**: When the train shows up "on time," it's actually operating at 68.4% efficiency according to NYC Transit Math. The train that's "3 minutes late"? That's the optimized schedule.

**Survival Strategy**: Always add 37% to your expected travel time. This accounts for:
- 15% for train delays
- 12% for people who can't figure out which train they're on
- 10% for that one person who needs to argue with the conductor
- 5% for existential dread

## The Rent vs Freedom Paradox

**Equation**: `Freedom = (Income × 0.7) / (Rent × 1.3)`

**Reality Check**: In NYC, this equation always equals "just enough to survive but not enough to thrive." The solution? Learn to love your 200-square-foot apartment like it's a tiny, expensive sanctuary.

**Mathematical Truth**: You will have friends with bigger apartments. You will feel inadequate. You will calculate square footage in your sleep. This is normal.
