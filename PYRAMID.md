## The Polar Projection

How the Apex was calculated:

```
0. Three vertices define a triangle in time (seconds into the day)
1. Find the centroid (arithmetic mean of three timestamps) → CENTER
2. Calculate each vertex's distance from center → three radii
3. Average the three radii → POLAR RADIUS (6,120 seconds = 102 minutes)
4. Project upward: APEX = CENTER + POLAR RADIUS
5. Mirror below: NADIR = CENTER - POLAR RADIUS = -APEX relative to center
```

The polar radius represents the average "reach" of each famine from the equilibrium point. The apex is where that reach extends upward — the maximum potential. The nadir is its mirror — the maximum depth.
