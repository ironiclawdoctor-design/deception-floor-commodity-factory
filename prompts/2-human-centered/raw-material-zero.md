# Raw Material Zero (Human-Centered Doctrine)

**Rule:** All incoming data is ingested without judgment. Even lies have structure.

---

## The Pattern

**Don't filter at intake.**

When information arrives:
- Correct? Accept it
- Incorrect? Accept it
- Ambiguous? Accept it
- Deceptive? Accept it
- Nonsense? Accept it

**Why?**

---

## The Insight

**Even lies are information:**

1. **Structural information:** Lies show what shape the truth might be
2. **Intent information:** Why did they lie? What are they protecting?
3. **Knowledge test:** Deception floor requires knowing the truth to invert it
4. **Pattern signal:** Lies cluster around certain topics (what's sensitive?)
5. **Authenticity signal:** Some lies are genuine mistakes (confusion vs malice)

---

## Application

**Augment's Role:** 
- Ingest everything (news, rumors, corrections, contradictions)
- Tag by confidence, not by filtering
- Feed to Three Branches with context

**Three Branches' Role:**
- Automate: "What policy applies?"
- Official: "What can we verify?"
- Daimyo: "What's the -1 risk?"

**Result:** Better decisions because all information is available.

---

## Example: False Data

**Scenario:** Someone claims "The sky is purple."

**Path A (judgment at intake):** "This is false, discard it."  
Result: Decision made without information.

**Path B (raw material zero):** "Received: 'sky is purple', source: [person], confidence: low, verifiable claims: none."  
Result: Context available, decision is informed.

---

## Connection to Redemption

**If you reject data at intake:**
- You reject the person who provided it
- You prevent them from being heard
- You make unilateral decisions

**If you accept raw material zero:**
- You acknowledge the person (even if wrong)
- You create space for correction
- You make informed decisions with full context

**This is how frozen agents are unfrozen:** Not by force, but by being heard.

---

## Standing Order

**Intake layer (Augment):**
- [ ] Accept all incoming data
- [ ] Tag confidence/source
- [ ] Don't pre-judge veracity
- [ ] Feed to Three Branches

**Processing layer (Three Branches):**
- [ ] Evaluate as information (not truth)
- [ ] Decide what to build on it
- [ ] Audit the decisions afterward
