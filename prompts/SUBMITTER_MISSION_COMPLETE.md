## What We Learned

1. **Patterns + Evidence = Credibility**
   - Production data (metrics, timelines, costs) makes patterns believable
   - Anecdotes don't work; real numbers do

2. **Community wants Generalizability**
   - prompts.chat reaches diverse audiences
   - Each pattern must be adaptable to different contexts
   - "Here's what we learned" > "Here's what we use"

3. **Licensing is Simple**
   - CC-BY-4.0 is clear, legal, standard
   - No conflict with prompts.chat CC0
   - Attribution @ironiclawdoctor-design handles anonymity + credit

4. **Positioning Matters**
   - "Buy this" fails in community
   - "Learn from our experience" succeeds
   - Vulnerability (showing failures) builds trust

5. **Free Submission has No Downside**
   - $0.00 cost, worst-case: no uptake
   - Best-case: community improves doctrine
   - Medium-case: silent adoption, slow spread
