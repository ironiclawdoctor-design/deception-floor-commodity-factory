## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Violates IP Confidentiality
**Mitigation:** All patterns are generalizable doctrines, not specific implementations. No confidential data exposed. CC-BY-4.0 license is permissive, not restrictive.

### Risk 2: Community rejects patterns
**Mitigation:** That's OK! Rejection = feedback. We learn what doesn't resonate. No reputational damage (anonymous contribution is possible if needed).

### Risk 3: Patterns are viewed as marketing
**Mitigation:** Frame as "we learned these from experience." No product plugs. No calls to action except "try this." "Tell us what you think."

### Risk 4: Licensing confusion
**Mitigation:** CC-BY-4.0 is clear, legal, standard in open-source. All submissions include license statement.

## Community Engagement Guidelines

### When Responding to Feedback

**Good response:**
"You're right, this pattern assumes X but doesn't explain why. Let me clarify..."

"I disagree, but I appreciate the perspective. Have you seen this failure mode?..."

"Great idea. We tested variant B and found A worked better for our constraints, but yours might fit your context better."

**Bad response:**
"You're wrong."

"This is obviously correct."

"We don't take feedback on doctrine."
