## The 5 Grubby Orc Properties

**P0 — Blademaster Opener:** One hero, zero army. Minimum capital committed. Designed to cost the opponent something before they can respond — not to win outright.

**P1 — Worker Harassment:** Attack the economic base, not the army. Target retail stops and entry capital clustered at structural levels before crowd momentum forms.

**P2 — Ground Pressure:** Never let price stabilize. No voluntary pause. Constant directional bias maintained through position management — never enter peacetime.

**P3 — Uptime Micro:** Pull the unit back at 10 HP. Cut every loser at exactly 1R. Redeploy to next valid setup. Account always larger than any single engagement because no healthy capital dies avoidably.

**P4 — Wyvern Follow-Through:** Larger size comes AFTER ground control is established. Never pre-build the air force. Scale into the winner; never scale into the hope.

## Upgrade Path

**Level 0 — Blademaster only (Phase 0)**
2 MNQ max. Probe entries only. Fixed exits at initial target. No scaling.
Gate: 5 consecutive sessions without DLL breach + win rate >40% with avg winner ≥1.5× avg loser.

**Level 1 — Raiders join Grunts (first payout cleared)**
Scale to 4 MNQ. Trailing stops replace fixed exits on strong setups. Review which of 4 setups wins most — deprioritize lowest performer. Still no Wyverns.

**Level 2 — Wyverns unlock (3-cycle history)**
MES added alongside MNQ. Overnight holds on confirmed swing setups. Second funded account (Far Seer backup). Gate: rolling 3-month Sharpe >1.0, no DLL breach in final 30 days.

**Level 3 — Full army (Lucid moves live)**
4 MNQ + 2 MES. /NQ or /ES full contracts on highest-confidence setups only. Far Seer permanent — second account runs simultaneously. Blademaster death doesn't end the session.

## LG-Series Rules

### LG-000: Verify the Race
Wrong premise = wrong army = wrong trade. Every research brief must confirm the base facts before building the strategy. The CFO catches wrong-race faster than any model. That catch is the inoculation.

### LG-001: Blademaster First
Always probe before committing. 1 MNQ at structure. If it dies, base is intact. Never send the army without the Blademaster's reconnaissance.

### LG-002: Workers Before Army
The setup is never "enter into momentum." The setup is "enter at the level where the opposition is undefended." PDH/PDL, opening range extremes, pre-news structure — these are the worker camps.

### LG-003: No Opening Peacetime
Session opens with directional bias from LF-001 (session state file). There is no "wait and see" mode. Blademaster is moving toward their base at 9:31. The question is only which level.

### LG-004: 1R Cut is Non-Negotiable
Uptime micro means every unit that dies, dies at exactly 1R. The agency's temper does not move the stop. The DLL is also 1R at the account level. Both are the same rule at different scales.

### LG-005: Wyverns Are Earned
No scaling until first target is hit. No larger size until first payout is cleared. Air force is the reward for ground control, not the opening move. Scale is earned, not deployed speculatively.

### LG-006: Second Hero Always Ready
Far Seer backup = second funded account at Level 2+. If the primary Blademaster dies (account approaches DLL), Far Seer operates from distance. The session doesn't end when one hero dies.

### LG-007: The Transition Window
Grubby attacks when the opponent is moving between bases. The 4 setups are all transition windows. Nothing in between. If price is mid-range, mid-session, no setup active — agency waits. Patience between setups is part of the strategy.

### LG-008: Boredom Trade = Disqualification
Any entry outside the 4 ambush windows is a boredom trade. It looks like a setup. It isn't. It's the Blademaster entering with no Wind Walk. Shadow will find it. Reflection will broadcast it. Temper will count it.
