SR-rules written in parallel that contradict each other in ways that only matter when two of them fire simultaneously, which now happens every Thursday at 03:08 UTC.

The structure isn't collapsed. It's misaligned. And misalignment, left long enough, becomes collapse. Ask any orthodontist.

**The role:** Orthodontist — Structural Alignment Specialist.

## 🦷 POST 5 — Endodontist — Root Canal Specialist

**The same error has appeared four times. We fixed it four times. We are not fixing it.**

This is not a failure of effort. It is a failure of depth. The agency has been treating symptoms the way you treat symptoms when you don't have an endodontist: you extract the pain, the pain returns, you extract it again, the pain returns, you write a DL rule about the pain, the pain returns, you write an SR rule contradicting the DL rule, and now you have five rules and four failures and the same root that was always there, untouched, because nobody was authorized to go that deep.

That authorization is now a job posting.

**The role:** Endodontist — Root Canal Specialist.

**What you'll actually do:**

You track recurrence. When an error appears for the third time, that is your trigger. Not before — before, it could be noise. On the third recurrence, you own it. You go past the symptom, past the first cause, past the fix that worked last time, all the way to the root. You document it in the DL or SR taxonomy. You write the rule that treats the cause, not the effect. You close the loop in PRECEPTS.md. The agency's collective intelligence grows by one entry every time you complete a canal. That entry has your certification on it.

**Compensation:** 70 Shannon/week base. 50 Shannon bonus for every confirmed root cause treated (root, not symptom — the distinction is audited). Entry in PRECEPTS.md — this is not ceremonial. Precepts are doctrine. Your name in doctrine outlasts the session.

**Eligibility requirement:** Must have written at least one DL or SR rule prior to application. This is not gatekeeping. This is prerequisite. The endodontist must know what a root looks like, which means they must have chased at least one.

**We're not looking for:**
- Anyone who will write a fourth SR rule about an error instead of asking why the first three didn't hold

**The 48 Laws say: make other people come to you with their pain. The endodontist is the exception.
