# Stripe Affiliate Program Overview

## How Stripe Affiliate Works

Stripe's affiliate program is a simple revenue-sharing model: when a customer signs up for a Stripe account via your referral link and processes payments, you earn a commission on their activity.

**Key Facts:**
- **Commission Rate:** 25-30% on Stripe's processing fees for the first year; ongoing commissions available for subscriptions
- **Commission Accrual:** Commissions accumulate in your Stripe affiliate dashboard and are paid out monthly
- **Eligibility:** Available in 15+ countries (US, UK, EU, Canada, Australia, etc.)
- **Sign-up:** Instant approval for most applicants; no separate agreement required

---

## Commission Structure

### Earning Model

| Referral Type | Commission | Duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **New Stripe Account** | 25-30% of fees | Year 1 | Highest earning period |
| **Ongoing Subscriptions** | 10-15% of fees | Indefinite | Recurring revenue from referred merchants |
| **SaaS/Marketplace Platforms** | 15-20% | Year 1+ | Higher commission for platform integrations |

### Example Earnings

**Scenario:** You refer a small business that processes $10,000/month in payments.
- Stripe's average fee: 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction (~2.7% total on volume)
- Monthly fees paid by merchant: ~$270
- Your commission (25%): **$67.50/month**
- Year 1 total: **$810**

**Scenario 2:** Medium business at $100,000/month volume
- Monthly fees: ~$2,700
- Your commission (25%): **$675/month**
- Year 1 total: **$8,100**

## What Counts Toward Commission

### ✅ Commission-Eligible Activities

1. **New Stripe Accounts** — Referred merchant signs up for their first Stripe account
2. **Payment Processing** — Commissions accrue as they process transactions
3. **Subscriptions** — Billing.com integrations, subscription platforms
4. **Add-on Products** — Stripe Tax, Sigma, Radar fraud prevention (higher margins)
5. **Geographic Expansion** — Refer new Stripe accounts in any supported country

### ❌ Non-Eligible Activities

- Existing Stripe users switching affiliate links (no credit)
- Internal transactions or test accounts
- Duplicate referrals from same visitor (first link wins)

## Payment Timing & Mechanics

### Monthly Payout Cycle

1. **Accrual Period:** Commissions accrue daily based on referral customer activity
2. **Payout Window:** Paid on the 15th of each month for activity from the prior month
3. **Minimum Threshold:** Most programs require ~$100+ minimum before payout (varies by region)
4. **Payment Method:** Direct ACH transfer to your bank account (US); international wire available
5. **Holding Period:** None — payouts are automatic once threshold is met

### Tracking

- Real-time dashboard showing:
  - Active referrals
  - Monthly commission accrual
  - Payout history
  - Click-through rates
  - Conversion rates

## Tax Implications

### United States

- **Form 1099-MISC:** Stripe issues if annual commissions exceed $600
- **Self-Employment Tax:** Commissions are treated as self-employment income (Schedule C, Form 1040)
- **Estimated Tax:** Quarterly payments recommended if significant commission income
- **Deductibility:** Marketing/promotion expenses are deductible

### Disclosure Requirements

- **FTC Compliance:** You must clearly disclose affiliate relationship wherever links appear
- **Statement Example:** "We earn a commission if you sign up via this link at no extra cost to you."
- **Best Practice:** Disclose at the point of recommendation, not hidden in footer

### International (Canada, EU, UK)

- **Canada:** Reported on T776 (business income); GST/HST may apply
- **EU:** VAT treatment varies by country; typically treated as service income
- **UK:** Self-assessment tax return required; tracked as miscellaneous income
- **Australia:** Reported on tax return; no separate threshold (any amount)
