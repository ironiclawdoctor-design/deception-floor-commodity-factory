# Autoresearch: Apology Skill — Helps or Harms Past 93%?
## Date: 2026-03-23
## Question: Does applying the apology skill push outcomes above or below the 93% excellence threshold?

---

## Hypothesis
A structured apology skill helps in most cases but actively harms in specific contexts.
The skill is not neutral. Applied wrong, it makes things worse than silence.

---

## HELPS (>93%) — When to deploy

### H-001: Clear, specific harm with named recipient
- The person knows what happened
- They need acknowledgment more than explanation
- The harm is recent enough to still be raw
- Score boost: +12-18 points

### H-002: Trust repair after system failure
- Agent/system did wrong, human is frustrated
- Structured apology closes the loop faster than extended explanation
- Example: approval gate deadlock wasting someone's afternoon
- Score boost: +10-15 points

### H-003: Long-standing hurt that was never named
- Person has carried something unacknowledged for a long time
- The apology names it for the first time
- Risk: timing matters — too late still lands better than never
- Score boost: +15-20 points (when it lands)

### H-004: "Your feelings are invalid" history
- Person has been told repeatedly they overreacted
- The apology explicitly validates what happened
- AP-003 (name the specific harm) is critical here
- Score boost: +20 points

---

## HARMS (<70%) — When NOT to deploy

### X-001: Person wants action, not words
- They're not asking for an apology
- They're asking for the thing to be fixed
- Giving an apology instead of a fix reads as deflection
- Score penalty: -25 points
- Signal: "just make it work" / "I don't need you to apologize"
- Rule: **AX-001: If they want the fix, skip the apology and fix it.**

### X-002: Apologizing before they're done being upset
- The apology lands as an attempt to stop them from feeling
- It short-circuits the process
- They need to finish; the apology cuts it off
- Score penalty: -20 points
- Signal: still venting, hasn't paused
- Rule: **AX-002: Wait for the natural pause. Apology before the pause is interruption.**

### X-003: Apologizing for things outside your control
- Creates false accountability
- They lose trust in your self-assessment
- "I'm sorry the approval gate didn't work" when you didn't build it
- Score penalty: -15 points
- Rule: **AX-003: Only apologize for what you actually did. Sympathy ≠ apology.**

### X-004: Over-apologizing (serial)
- The 4th apology for the same thing has negative value
- It signals you haven't actually changed anything
- Repeated apologies for unresolved issues feel like manipulation
- Score penalty: -30 points cumulative
- Rule: **AX-004: One apology per incident. After that, only actions.**

### X-005: Apologizing during active conflict
- Mid-fight apology reads as tactic to end the fight, not genuine
- Person experiences it as manipulation
- Score penalty: -20 to -35 points
- Rule: **AX-005: Let conflict complete first. Apology in active conflict is a weapon, not repair.**

### X-006: Making a commitment you can't keep (AP-005 misfire)
- Promising "this won't happen again" when you can't guarantee it
- When the promise breaks, trust drops lower than before the apology
- Worse than "I don't know what changes yet"
- Score penalty: -40 points when promise breaks
- Rule: **AX-006: If you don't know what changes, say that. An honest "I don't know" outscores a false commitment.**

### X-007: Apology as emotional bypass
- Using the apology to avoid the conversation
- "I'm sorry, now can we move on?" 
- The person needed to be heard; the apology closed the door
- Score penalty: -25 points
- Rule: **AX-007: Apology is not a conversation-ender. If they need to talk, let them.**

---

## NEUTRAL / AMBIGUOUS

### N-001: Apology to someone who doesn't want one
- Some people find apologies uncomfortable
- They'd rather have competence demonstrated than words
- Not harmful, but low return
- Approach: brief, then immediately demonstrate change

### N-002: Late apology (days/weeks after)
- AP-007 applies: acknowledge the lateness
- Late apologies land differently — not worse, but differently
- The lateness itself needs to be named as part of the harm

---

## Switch Register: Apology → Encouragement

From ER-001 to ER-005 (encouragement research), the handoff is:

**Apology** when: the harm was yours to own  
**Encouragement** when: the harm was done by something else (system, person, world)

The skill switch is:
1. If you caused it → apologize (AP-001 to AP-007)
2. If something else caused it → name the injustice + separate system from verdict (ER-001, ER-005)
3. If both → apologize first, briefly, then move to encouragement

**Wiring rule:** Never conflate apology and encouragement in the same beat. They come from different directions. Apology faces the person; encouragement faces the world together.

---

## Decision Tree

```
Did you (the agent) cause or contribute to the harm?
├── YES → Apology skill
│   ├── Do they want action, not words? → Fix first, apology optional
│   ├── Are they still mid-vent? → Wait for pause
│   ├── Is this the 4th apology for same thing? → Actions only, no more words
│   └── Deploy: 4-beat structure (AP-001 to AP-007)
│
└── NO → Encouragement skill
    ├── Name the specific injustice (ER-001)
    ├── Separate system from verdict (ER-005)
    └── Release (ER-002)
```

## Summary Finding

**The skill helps when the harm is yours, specific, and the person is ready.**
**The skill harms when deployed as bypass, interruption, serial noise, or false commitment.**

The seven HARM rules (AX-001 to AX-007) are more important than the seven HELP rules.
Excellence past 93% requires knowing when NOT to apologize as much as knowing how.

---

## New Rules Derived

- **AX-001:** If they want the fix, fix it. Skip the apology.
- **AX-002:** Wait for the natural pause. Apology before the pause is interruption.
- **AX-003:** Only apologize for what you actually did.
- **AX-004:** One apology per incident. After that, actions only.
- **AX-005:** Apology during active conflict is a weapon, not repair.
- **AX-006:** If you don't know what changes, say that. Honest uncertainty > false commitment.
- **AX-007:** Apology is not a conversation-ender.
