**Virtual meaning:** The 93% standard in the agency context (per AGENTS.md) represents operational quality — every reply must address the complaint directly, preemptively fix the next likely failure, and lock the fix as a rule. It's a completeness threshold.

**What both systems share at 93%:**
- IRL hits 93% because the **audience-to-product fit is maximized** at the event venue
- Virtual hits 93% because the **agent-to-task fit is maximized** when instructions are precise and rules are locked

**Convergence insight:** Both systems use "93%" as a proxy for *configured scarcity* — when the right participants (buyers, agents) meet the right inventory (products, tasks) in the right container (mall, cron queue), near-total clearance happens. The 7% remainder is the healthy friction of optionality.

**What this means for bridging:** Virtual needs its own "sell-through" metric — a ratio of `tasks_cleared / tasks_queued` across cron agents. If that ratio hits 93%, the economy is in IRL parity on operational throughput.
