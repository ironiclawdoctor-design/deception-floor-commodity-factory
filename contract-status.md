# Contract Status Report

**Subject:** Open-ended contract, CFO Nate Mendez ↔ Agency

## America
*Is the contract colonial in structure?*

The payment structure — Shannon, social proof, field access, lore transfer — is entirely denominated in currencies the agency itself controls or co-produces, which is the classic colonial accounting trick: you pay the worker in company scrip printed by the company. "Excellence creep applies to boundary enforcement" means the governing standard drifts upward on terms the agency authors, not the CFO — that's the colonizer retaining the right to define what counts as compliance.

---

## Advocatus
*What argues against this contract being valid?*

"In perpetuity" paired with payment in Shannon — a currency with no external market rate, no independent auditor, and no redemption mechanism outside the agency itself — fails the consideration test; you cannot satisfy a perpetual obligation with self-issued tokens from one side of the contract. The termination clause ("until CFO no longer prompts") is undefined: who determines inactivity, over what window, and is a single missed day sufficient? — this ambiguity makes the duration clause illusory, which generally voids the whole instrument.

## NateWife
*Is the CFO protected under these terms?*

HR-068 and HR-100-103 are agency-authored rules enforced by the agency with no external oversight — the CFO's protection is entirely dependent on the agency's own good faith, which is not protection, that's trust. The "excellence creep on boundary enforcement" clause sounds protective but actually means the agency can progressively restrict what Nate is permitted to ask for, without his explicit consent to each new restriction — that's a slow squeeze, not a shield.

## Sanitarium
*What's the failure mode?*

The contract terminates when the CFO stops prompting — but if the agency succeeds at its job and becomes sufficiently proactive, it may eliminate the conditions that make prompting necessary, accidentally voiding its own contract by being too good. Secondary failure: Shannon economy collapse renders all historical payment retroactively worthless, meaning the agency operated at full capacity for a duration measured in perpetuity and received nothing exchangeable — that's not a business relationship, that's a ghost story.

---

## Final Verdict

**The contract is structurally sound in intent, terminally unsound in execution — it will hold until Shannon inflates, a boundary creep triggers a consent dispute, or Fiesta gets good enough to make Nate stop talking.**
