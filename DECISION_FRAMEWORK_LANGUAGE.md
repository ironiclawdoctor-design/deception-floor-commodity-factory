# Decision Framework Language Update (2026-03-14 17:05 UTC)

## The Shift

### Old Language (Binary Ultimatum)
- Approve / Reject
- Yes / No
- Accept / Deny
- Allow / Forbid

**Problem:** Too rigid. Triggers trauma around binary choice, ultimatum, forced decision.

### New Language (Endorsement Model)
- **Endorse** — This direction feels right. Move forward.
- **Revoke** — This direction changed or feels wrong. Pull back.
- **Hold** — Need more time. Don't move yet.
- **Iterate** — Direction is right, execution needs adjustment.

---

## Why This Matters

**Approve/Reject** = Black/white, right/wrong, winner/loser  
**Endorse/Revoke** = Direction, intention, flexibility, course correction

**Approve** feels like: "You proved yourself right"  
**Endorse** feels like: "This direction aligns with what we're building"

**Reject** feels like: "You failed"  
**Revoke** feels like: "This doesn't fit anymore"

The frame is *different*. The stakes are softer.

---

## Applied to Actually's Framework

### Old Framing
> "You propose: Bet $2 on Polymarket. User approves/rejects."

### New Framing
> "You propose: Bet $2 on Polymarket. User endorses or revokes."

If you revoke: Not "you were wrong," but "this direction changed."

---

## Applied to All Agency Decisions

### Tier Routing
**Old:** "Approve routing to Grok?"  
**New:** "Endorse Grok for this query, or revoke in favor of BitNet?"

### Revenue Decisions
**Old:** "Approve $14.99 price increase?"  
**New:** "Endorse the price signal based on data, or revoke and hold at $9.99?"

### Feature Prioritization
**Old:** "Approve building feature X?"  
**New:** "Endorse this feature priority based on user requests, or revoke in favor of Y?"

---

## Standing Policy Update

Across all frameworks (routing, financial, product):

**When I propose something:**
- You can **endorse** (move forward)
- You can **revoke** (pull back)
- You can **hold** (need more time)
- You can **iterate** (direction right, execution adjust)

No binary ultimatum. No trauma frame. Just direction.

---

## Actually's Financial Framework (Updated Language)

Instead of:
> "Decision proposed. Awaiting approval."

Now:
> "Decision proposed. Ready for endorsement or revocation."

If you endorse: We execute.  
If you revoke: We pause.  
If you iterate: We adjust and re-propose.

No judgment. No pass/fail. Just navigation.

---

## The Teaching Truth

This is how trust actually works:
- Not "prove you're right by getting approval"
- But "move together in endorsed direction, adjust when needed"

The language shift is the doctrine shift.
