## VALUATION METHODOLOGY (How Sharks Think)

Shark Tank sharks apply the following to pre-revenue software/AI companies:

**1. Berkus Method (pre-revenue tech):**
Assigns up to $500K value per factor:
- Sound idea / proof of concept: up to $500K
- Prototype / working product: up to $500K
- Quality management team / documented systems: up to $500K
- Strategic relationships / traction signals: up to $500K
- Product rollout / first revenue: up to $500K
- **Maximum Berkus value: $2.5M**

**2. Comparable Transaction / Comparable Company:**
AI agent infrastructure companies (2024-2025 vintage) are trading at 20-50x ARR at seed/pre-seed. For pre-revenue, proxies used:
- Cost-to-replicate: what would it cost a competitor to build the same stack?
- Strategic value: what is the system worth to a buyer who wants the capability NOW?

**3. VC Scorecard:**
Adjusts a regional baseline ($1.5M for pre-seed software) up/down based on:
- Team strength, product readiness, market size, competition, partner readiness, IP

**4. First Chicago / DCF:**
Rarely used on Shark Tank for pre-revenue. Sharks dismiss projections. They anchor to: 
- "What do you have TODAY that is real?" + "What multiple am I buying on future revenue?"

**Key Shark patterns:**
- Mark Cuban: pays for IP + distribution leverage. Wants ownership % that justifies his network.
- Kevin O'Leary (Mr. Wonderful): demands royalty structures when ARR is speculative.
- Lori Greiner: consumer-accessible products. Less relevant.
- Daymond John: brand + community plays. Would like the Shannon economy narrative.
- Robert Herjavec: enterprise tech + cybersecurity. Will respond to the agent security doctrine.
