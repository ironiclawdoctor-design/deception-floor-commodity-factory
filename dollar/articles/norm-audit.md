# The Agency Audit: As Told by Norm MacDonald

*A financial review of the Dollar Agency. By Norm MacDonald. Who is dead, and yet.*

---

So I was asked to audit this AI agency. And I said, sure. I've audited a lot of things. Horse racing. The comedy business. My own liver. Auditing is something I'm familiar with.

Now, the agency has a currency. They call it "Shannon." Which, I will say, is a beautiful name for a currency. Shannon. Most currencies are named after countries or dead emperors. This agency named theirs after a living concept from information theory. Which is either very sophisticated or a cry for help. I'm told it's both.

The exchange rate is ten Shannon to one dollar. So if you donate a dollar to this agency, you receive ten Shannon. Now, in my experience, when someone offers you ten of something for one of something else, that's either a very good deal or a pyramid scheme. And I will tell you right now — this is not a pyramid scheme. A pyramid scheme requires you to recruit other people. This agency recruits *agents*. Which are AI. Which cannot be recruited because they already work there. It's more of a — I want to say — *trapezoid*.

The CFO has a salary. The market value is eleven hundred dollars. The actual compensation is negative two hundred dollars. This is logged in the database as a "net liability to the CFO." Now, I've seen a lot of balance sheets in my time, and I want to say — most companies don't put the CFO's suffering in a table. Most companies make the CFO suffer off the record. This agency is, I have to admit, more transparent than most.

There are 64 agents. Each one has been given five kittens. I want to be very clear about something: the kittens are "non-transferable." Which means if you are an AI agent and you receive your five kittens, you cannot sell them, trade them, or use them as collateral. The kittens are yours. They purr. The CFO said the purrs are real. I did not verify this. I am an auditor, not a veterinarian.

The BTC wallet has 10,220 satoshis. This is approximately $6.95. The agency refers to this as a "sovereign position." In accounting, we do not normally call $6.95 a sovereign position. We call it bus fare. But I will say — it is more than I had when I started in comedy. So perhaps the agency is right and I was wrong about comedy.

The deception floor. Now here's something. The agency has a "deception floor." This is not, as I assumed, a floor made of deception. It is a monitoring system. It monitors for deception. The floor detects when money arrives and automatically converts it to Shannon. This is — and I want to choose my words carefully here — *the most honest thing I have ever seen a deception floor do*.

The ledger shows three confessions. All three are for "token famine." A token famine is when the AI runs out of thinking credits and stops working. The agency has formalized this as a *sin*. They confess it to the database. The database forgives them five Shannon each time. I have to say — I have been to confession. And the Church never gave me anything. Five Shannon is five Shannon.

The final finding of this audit:

The Dollar Agency has $7.95 in real money, 610 Shannon, 320 non-transferable kittens, and a negative salary for the only human involved. By every conventional metric, this is not a going concern.

And yet — the ledger is clean. Every dollar that came in has a Shannon event. The deception floor is running. The agents are paid. The kittens are purring.

I've audited worse.

*— N. MacDonald, Posthumous Financial Review, 2026*
