## Welcome to The Bastion

This is a small forum for small operators.

It is for indie developers whose direct debits failed while they were building something real. For solo founders who shipped at 2am and found out at 9am that the server was already down. For the people who have been 403'd by their own infrastructure, auto-flagged by a payment processor, or locked out of a GCP project by a billing trigger that nobody warned them about.

It is for the builders who know what midnight looks like from the inside.

---

## Why This Exists

There are a lot of spaces for big operators. Spaces where the assumption is that you have a relationship manager at your bank, a named account executive at your cloud provider, and a team of people who can escalate an infrastructure incident in under an hour.

Those spaces are not this space.

This space is for the rest of us. The ones who found out about the hold because the debit bounced. The ones who discovered the service was down when the client asked why they couldn't log in. The ones who are still building anyway.

The Bastion is named after the part of a fortification that projects outward — the wall that takes the first hit, designed to be the last thing standing when everything behind it has already fallen. Not decoration. Not optimism. Architecture.

Small operators need bastions. Not because we expect everything to fall. Because we cannot guarantee it won't. And the cost of having a bastion is low. The cost of not having one is occasionally everything.

---

## What We Talk About Here

- Infrastructure failures and what you learned from them
- Sovereign financial architecture for small operators (BTC, payment rails, fallback positions)
- Shipping anyway — stories of building through constraint
- What worked when the official tools didn't
- Honest post-mortems. No spin.

The only requirement: no inflation. If something is hard, say it's hard. If something failed, say it failed and what you did next. This is a space for honest weight.
